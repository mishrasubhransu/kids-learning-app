# ToddLearn - Potential Feature List

## High Impact - Engagement & Retention

1. **Progress tracking & streaks** - Save quiz scores per child to Supabase. Show a simple dashboard with stars/stickers earned. Daily streaks ("3 days in a row!") are powerful motivators even for toddlers (parents love seeing them too).

2. **Achievement badges** - "Learned all animals!", "Got 10 in a row!", "Tried every category". Visual reward collection screen. Kids love collecting things.

3. **Multi-child profiles** - Parents often have more than one kid. Let them switch between profiles so progress is tracked per child.

4. **Adaptive difficulty** - Track what the child gets wrong and serve those items more frequently. If they always nail "A" but miss "Q", show "Q" more. Simple spaced-repetition lite.

## High Impact - New Learning Modes

5. **Matching game** - Show a grid of face-down cards, flip to match pairs (letter to letter, shape to shape, animal to its name). Classic memory game, very age-appropriate.

6. **Tracing/drawing mode** - Let kids trace letters and numbers with their finger on the touchscreen. Even rough path detection would be engaging.

7. **Counting game** - Show N objects on screen, ask "how many?" with number buttons. Goes beyond just showing numbers to teaching actual counting.

8. **Sorting/categorizing** - "Which of these is a fruit?" / "Which is a shape?" - drag items into buckets. Cross-category learning.

9. **Spelling mode** - Show a picture of an apple, then scrambled letters A-P-P-L-E to arrange. Start with 3-letter words for younger kids.

## Medium Impact - Content Expansion

10. **Sight words / simple words** - "cat", "dog", "the", "and" - a natural progression from letters.

11. **Body parts** - Head, hands, eyes, nose. Very relevant for toddlers, easy to illustrate.

12. **Opposites** - Big/small, hot/cold, up/down. Pairs well with the existing tile view.

13. **Emotions/feelings** - Happy, sad, angry, surprised. Social-emotional learning is huge at this age.

14. **Days of the week / weather** - Builds routine awareness.

## Medium Impact - Experience Polish

15. **PWA / offline mode** - Parents use this in cars, waiting rooms, planes. Offline support would be a killer feature. Service worker + cached assets.

16. **Multi-language support** - Even just adding Spanish or Mandarin would open up a huge audience. The data structure already supports it (add a translations map).

17. **Parent dashboard** - Simple page showing: time spent, categories practiced, quiz accuracy over time, weakest areas. Doesn't need to be fancy - a few charts.

18. **Customizable rewards** - Let parents upload a photo as the "reward" after completing a quiz (e.g., a picture of grandma, their pet). Extremely personal motivation.

19. **Sound effects & animations** - Confetti on correct answers, a bounce animation on tiles, a celebratory sound on streak milestones. Small polish, big delight.

## Lower Effort / Quick Wins

20. **Dark mode / night mode** - Dimmer colors for bedtime learning sessions.

21. **Autoplay quiz mode** - Like autoplay in scroll mode but for tests - continuously serve questions without needing to restart.

22. **Category completion indicators** - On the home screen, show which categories have been fully explored or quizzed.

23. **"Word of the day"** - Simple feature on the home screen highlighting one item each day.

24. **Keyboard shortcuts cheat sheet** - For parents using desktop.

## Monetization-Adjacent

25. **Premium content packs** - Musical instruments, countries/flags, dinosaurs, space.

26. **Print worksheets** - Generate a PDF worksheet from quiz results for offline practice.

27. **Weekly email to parents** - Summary of what the child learned that week.
